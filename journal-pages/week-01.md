## *Experiment 1: Data Drawings*

In a group of 5 people, we collected personal data from one another and collaborated on
a hand-drawn data visualisation to produce a “group portrait” made entirely from
data. We then swaped our portraits and try to decode what each visualisation
reveals about the people behind it.

## Individual work:

Another task we had to do in our own time was to choose a topic, and pick an aspect of your daily life that you are curious about but don't normally pay
close attention to. It should be something you can observe and record by hand over
the course of 4–5 days.
I chose to observe what apps I went on my phone at what time, and how I felt emotionally afterwards.

### Step 2: Collect data by hand
I collected my data in a small note book over the course of 4 days. To make the data less chaotic I focused on the main apps (not apps like clock or photos app etc).

![alt text](IMG_0432-1.jpeg)

### Step 3: Design your visualisation
After the collection period, I created a data drawing on one side of A4 card.I invented a personal visual language: using colour, shape, and position to encode different aspects of my data. On the reverse side, I drew a 
legend that explains my visual system.
![alt text](IMG_0434-1.jpeg)
![alt text](IMG_0435-1.jpeg)


### Reflection

- What did you choose to track, and why? 

I decided to track what I did on my phone and at what times, and how I felt afterwards.

- What was it like to collect and visualise this data?

It was quite strange at first, as I felt self-conscious of when I would pick up my phone as I knew I would have to document it. Sometimes it was very easy to forget. It was interesting to see what apps I lent towards, and the patterns that I would have never noticed.

- What did you notice that you wouldn't have otherwise?

I never realised how often I checked my bank account, and how it was the main app that made me feel quite bad afterwards haha. It was interesting how I was quite neutral on most of my feelings, but using imessage made me feel the most happy, and I used it the most often! 

- What choices did you make for your data collection? What does it emphasise? What is left out?

I think this data collection made me really realise how much I use my phone, as I don't really think about this normally. Luckily I felt more good emotions over the bad, but I still had negative experiences, which makes me think I could be trying to use my phone only when neccessary. I think if I included how long I was on each app that would be interesting to see, or the purpose for why I used my phone in the first place!

- How does this exercise relate to data humanism and the *Dear Data* project?

This project related to data humanism and the *Dear Data* project, as it observes and documents not just numbers, but feelings. It is Human-Centric Data Representation: data that represents me and my experience, and involves interpreting data in a way that considers the emotional implications.
