## Documentation 

## 1. Action Plan
At this stage of the project, the core concept and interaction system were functioning successfully. The primary focus shifted from experimentation to refinement and completion. Reflecting on the project, I identified several areas that required attention before the final showcase:
- Complete the remaining interactive memory orbs.
- Improve the visual composition of the archive.
- Strengthen the illusion of scale.
- Refine the explanatory interface.
- Test interactions and fix errors.

Although I was unable to participate in the in-class critique activities, I critically reviewed my own project and identified similar issues that would likely arise during audience testing. In particular, I felt that the archive needed to feel larger and more immersive without becoming technically complex.
The challenge was finding ways to create the impression of a much larger dataset while working within the limited scope of the project.

## 2. Expanding the Archive
One of the most important developments this week involved increasing the perceived scale of the archive.
Although approximately 40–50 memories were interactive, I wanted the space to feel as though it contained many more memories beyond those immediately accessible to the viewer.
To achieve this, I duplicated and repositioned memory orbs throughout the environment.
Many of these additional orbs were placed:
- Above the player
- Beyond interaction range
- Around the perimeter of the space
- At varying heights and distances

This created a stronger sense that the archive continued indefinitely beyond the visible dataset.
The result was a space that felt much larger and more atmospheric without requiring hundreds of individual interactions.

## 3. Refining Data Representation
As I continued populating the archive, I refined the visual relationships between the dataset and the environment.
The final visual system became:
- Family memories = Pink
- Friend memories = Green
- Achievement memories = Blue
- Exercise memories = Yellow

Orb size was determined by age.
Smaller orbs represented younger participants, while larger orbs represented older participants.
This approach allowed multiple layers of information to be communicated simultaneously.
One of the strengths of this system was its simplicity. Viewers could immediately recognise patterns and differences without needing to read extensive explanations.

## 4. Interface Refinement
The explanatory interface also evolved during this stage.
Earlier versions focused primarily on displaying memory text. However, I realised that visitors needed additional context to understand how the archive functioned as a data visualisation.

To address this, I created informational panels explaining:
- The title of the project
- The meaning of orb colours
- The relationship between age and orb size
- Information about the HappyDB dataset

![alt text](<../assets/week-10/memory overlay_00047.png>)

Adding this information helped communicate the project's data-driven foundation while maintaining the visual simplicity of the archive.

## 5. Testing and Troubleshooting
A significant portion of this week involved testing interactions and correcting issues.
Because each memory orb required its own interaction, I had to ensure that:
- Memory text appeared correctly
- UI elements disappeared when expected
- Interactions remained consistent
- Colours remained accurate

While none of these issues were individually complex, the large number of interactive objects meant that small mistakes could quickly accumulate.
Testing became an important part of the process and helped improve the reliability of the final experience.

## Independent Study
### Project Development
This week focused almost entirely on completing the visualisation.
Unlike previous weeks, where much of my time was spent learning new software, I was now applying those skills to build and refine the final archive.
One of the most satisfying aspects of this stage was seeing individual elements come together into a cohesive experience. The environment, interactions, data mappings, and interface all began functioning as a unified system rather than separate experiments.

The archive now successfully allowed users to:
- Explore the space freely
- Identify categories through colour
- Understand age through size
- Interact with memories
- Read real experiences from the dataset

This represented the core goals I had established earlier in the project.

### Reflection
This week highlighted the importance of refinement within interactive projects.
Earlier development stages focused on solving technical problems and establishing functionality. However, small adjustments to layout, scale, composition, and interface design ultimately had a significant impact on the overall experience.
One of the most valuable lessons I learnt was that atmosphere can be created through relatively simple design decisions. The use of a dark background, glowing orbs, and spatial arrangement created a much stronger experience than a highly detailed environment would have.

If I had more time, I would have liked to introduce:
- Orb movement and animation
- Sound effects
- Additional visual feedback during interaction
- More complex data relationships
- A larger dataset

However, given my limited experience with Unreal Engine and the timeframe available, I was pleased with the outcome and proud that I had successfully built a functioning interactive visualisation.
The project had developed from a spreadsheet of memories into an explorable archive of human experiences, demonstrating how data can be transformed into a more emotional and immersive form.
