## Final Development Sprint
With the core archive completed, Week 11 focused on refinement, testing, and preparing the project for presentation. Unlike previous weeks, which involved major technical learning and experimentation, this stage was about improving the overall experience and ensuring the project communicated its ideas clearly.

One of my primary goals was reviewing the archive from the perspective of a first-time visitor. After spending so much time building the project, it became easy to assume that certain information was obvious when it actually needed additional explanation. I therefore revisited the interface and archive layout to ensure users could quickly understand how the visualisation worked.

The final archive consisted of approximately 40-50 curated memories drawn from the HappyDB dataset. Each memory was represented by a coloured orb, with category communicated through colour and age represented through scale. Users could move throughout the space and reveal memories through interaction.

## Refining the Environment
Although the environment was intentionally minimal, I spent time adjusting the placement of objects to improve visual balance.
I noticed that some sections of the archive felt visually empty while others appeared overcrowded. Small adjustments to the positioning of orbs helped create a stronger composition and encouraged viewers to explore multiple areas of the archive.
I also continued adding distant background orbs. While these objects were not interactive, they strengthened the illusion that the archive extended beyond the immediately visible memories.

This became an important design decision because it helped transform the archive from a collection of objects into a larger imagined system.

## Final Testing
Before finalising the project, I tested each interactive memory individually.
This involved checking:
- Trigger functionality
- Correct memory text
- UI appearance
- Colour consistency
- Object scaling
- Player navigation

Although the interaction system was relatively simple, testing revealed several small mistakes that would have negatively affected the experience if left unresolved.
Correcting these issues improved the overall reliability of the visualisation and made the final experience feel more polished.

## Reflection
Looking back on the project, one of the biggest challenges was learning Unreal Engine within a limited timeframe.
At the beginning of development, I had very little experience with Blueprint systems, interaction design, or environmental creation. Even seemingly simple tasks often required extensive experimentation and troubleshooting.

However, these challenges ultimately became one of the most valuable aspects of the project. Rather than relying on software I was already comfortable with, I chose to work within a tool that aligned more closely with my future interests in immersive storytelling, interactive environments, and game design.

Throughout the process I learnt:
- Basic Blueprint interaction systems
- Unreal Engine UI widgets
- Asset importing and scene construction
- Material creation
- Environmental composition
- Interactive experience design

While the final project remained relatively simple, the technical skills developed throughout the process will be directly relevant to future work, particularly my capstone project.

## What Worked Well
Several aspects of the project were particularly successful.
The visual language was easy to understand and allowed data to be communicated without relying heavily on text. The use of colour, scale, and spatial arrangement helped transform the dataset into an explorable experience rather than a traditional visualisation.

The decision to curate a smaller dataset also proved effective. Rather than attempting to visualise thousands of memories, focusing on approximately 50 entries created a more manageable and meaningful experience. Finally, the archive atmosphere helped reinforce the project's themes of memory, collection, and human experience.


## Future Improvements
If I were to continue developing the project beyond the course, I would expand both the technical complexity and the data representation.
Potential future developments include:
- Animated floating orb movement
- Dynamic lighting effects
- Audio feedback when interacting with memories
- Additional memory categories
- Larger datasets
- Search or filtering systems
- More sophisticated data relationships between memories

I would also like to explore how memories could be connected through emotional themes rather than only category-based organisation.
These additions could strengthen the archive's ability to communicate patterns within the dataset while creating a richer interactive experience.

## Preparation for Showcase
The final stage of this week involved preparing materials for presentation.
This included:
- Capturing screenshots
- Recording interaction footage
- Organising project documentation
- Refining the project statement
- Preparing final GitHub journal entries

At this point the project had reached a stage where it successfully communicated its central idea: transforming human memories into an interactive spatial archive.
The focus moving into Week 12 was therefore on presenting the project effectively and reflecting on the overall process.
